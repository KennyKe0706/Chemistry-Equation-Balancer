Chemistry Equation Balancer

Type something like Fe + O2 -> Fe2O3. It gives the smallest whole-number coefficients.

What it does: balances neutral equations by mass only. Works with parentheses and hydrates (· or .).
What it does not do: ions/charges, electrons, acidic/basic redox, state labels, multiple arrows.
Who this is for: intro chem (Gr 9–10). Probably not enough for most Gr 11–12/AP/IB.
How it works (short)
  • Counts elements in each formula (handles nested () and hydrates).
  • Makes a matrix for left vs right and solves one nullspace vector.
  • Scales to the smallest integers. Use one -> and + between items.

Tip: remove things like (aq) and don’t type leading numbers like 2H2O in the input.
